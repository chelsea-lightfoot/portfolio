Data Overview Dashboards

Process

The homepage of our application was fairly empty, and failed to provide system administrators a view into the day-to-day activity of their company - data which we possessed, but had not surfaced through our application.

First and foremost, I had to identify the constraints of the tools I would be working with. I discussed what data was readily available with our engineering team and researched the Javascript library we were using for data visualization in other modules of the application. I researched what made a good software dashboard, and — with a spark of inspiration — went straight into Adobe Illustrator to create a prototype.

Half-way through my design, we had a major priortiy shift in the company. Inxeption had grown to over 200 customers and the small management application we had created was no longer powerful enough. As an integral part of this initiative to improve the management application, we wanted to provide our customers with the tools to view relevant data quickly and easily. We decided to re-use my original dashboard project with slightly different data.

I had many meetings with management to determine what data they wanted to see and what they considered most important. Then I reviewed the data requested with engineering to understand what was actually feasible in the short time we had allocated for this project. Armed with those competing interests, I was able to collaborate with another designer on my team to present relevant, high-priority data in my dashboard design.

Solution

Through the course of this project I created three dashboard designs: the original application dashboard, mini dashboards for the application modules, and the management tool overview dashboard that was implemented in the end.

While producing the design of these graphs and charts, I sought out opportunities for flexibility and the incorporation of future vision. For example, I built in room for notifications in our header and a product completion percentage graph - the latter of which has since been integrated into the design of the Product Details Input Flow.
