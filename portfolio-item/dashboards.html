Data Overview Dashboards

Problem

The homepage of our application was fairly empty, and we didnâ€™t have a way for managers of the system to get a quick look at the day to day activity of their company. We had a lot of data saved in the backend that we wanted to surface to the user.

Process

My first step was to talk to engineering about what data was available and what limitations we had. Then I researched the Javascript library we were using for data visualizations on the inner modules of the application so I could understand the limitations there as well. I researched what made a good software dashboard and with a spark of inspiration I went straight into Illustrator to create a prototype.

Half-way through We had a major priority shift in the company to respond to a new problem - Inxeption had grown to over 200 customers and the small management application we had created was no longer powerful enough. As a part of this new initiative to improve the management application, upper management wanted a place to view relevant data in this management tool quickly and easily. We decided to use my original dashboard project with slightly different data.

I had many meetings with management to determine what data they wanted to see and what they considered most important. Then I reviewed the data requested with engineering to understand what was actually feasible in the short amount of time given. With that I was able to collaborate with another designer on my team to present the data requested in the design I originally created.

Solution

Shown here are the various dashboard designs I created over the course of this project. There are three versions shown here: the original application dashboard, mini dashboards for the application modules, and the management tool overview dashboard that was implemented in the end.

Along with the look and feel of the basic graphs and tables, I ensured that this design could be easily updated over time as needs changed and the product expanded. I chose to work in some future vision as well, such as showing notifications and a percentage of completion graph for the products dashboard — a feature which has since been integrated into the design of the Product Details Input Flow.
