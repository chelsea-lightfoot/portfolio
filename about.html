Resume About Me

Hi there! My name is Chelsea Lightfoot

A designer of many talents, through my career of designing for web applications I have found that I excel at supporting developer efforts by taking their impressive work and turning it into an amazing experience.

Education

While I was studying Graphic Design at Cal Poly in San Luis Obispo, I befriended many people in the computer science department. When I graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) in 2014, not only did I have my knowledge of branding, web design, and print design, I also brought with me a strong understanding of the software engineering process and the challenges developers face.

Experience

I quickly became fluent in HTML and CSS so I could better support my freelance work — transferring those skills when I joined The Point Collective, a small design firm owned by veteran designers Jaime Merz and Mandi Chase. Our main objective was to support small businesses in building and maintaining an online presence — a complex process that I enjoyed simplifying through designing not only the website itself, but the system our clients could use to update their content as well. I took pride in empowering clients to take their online presence into their own hands.

After The Point Collective, I moved on to Inxeption as a UI/UX Designer where I have had the privilege to design for the application since its —ahem— inception. For the last two years I have been working to understand how to turn "boring" data entry and order tracking into a rewarding experience that encourages customers to use the system to its fullest. I now lead all design projects for our marketing team with a focus on the design of our website and how it integrates with our industrial commerce ecosystem.

Personality

I also enjoy front end development and I have become fluent in HTML, CSS / SASS, and Javascript / jQuery. I used those skills to build this website from scratch! I am currently working on a full web app project called the Oracle of Items - check it out to see my process and progress.

In my free time I like to listen to podcasts about true crime, comedy, and — another pastime of time — Dungeons and Dragons. I also play quite a few video games. Not only do I enjoy the games themselves, but I also love to learn about the design of rewarding gameplay mechanics. Outside of the tech world I express my creativity with resin casting and wearing various Japanese street fashions. I aspire to one day become a foster for cats and kittens in need, and to care for feral cat colonies in the Bay Area.
